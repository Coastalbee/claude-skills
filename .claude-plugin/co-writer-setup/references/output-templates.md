# Output Templates

Use these templates to generate the three context files. Every section must be filled with specific, personal content based on the interview. No placeholders. No generic descriptions. If a sentence could apply to anyone, rewrite it.

---

## USER.md Template

**Important:** USER.md is about who this person IS — their background, thinking, personality, values, and working style. It does NOT contain voice, tone, writing style, or formatting preferences. Those belong in a separate voice skill.

```markdown
# USER.md — [Their Name]

> Last updated: [today's date]

## Who I Am

[2–3 paragraph narrative about the person — their background, origin story, what they've built,
what drives them. Written in third person so Claude can reference it naturally.
Rich and specific — not a resume. This should feel like a real human portrait.]

## How I Think

[How they approach problems, make decisions, and process information. Are they systematic or
intuitive? Big-picture or detail-first? Fast-moving or methodical? Specific enough that Claude
can anticipate how they want to work through a problem before they explain it.]

## How I Work

[Working style and collaboration preferences. Do they want one strong recommendation or options?
Should Claude produce first and refine, or ask clarifying questions before starting?
How to handle uncertainty. What makes a session feel productive. What frustrates them.]

## What I Care About

[Their values and non-negotiables. What they're uncompromising about. What they stand for.
What they absolutely won't tolerate — in work, in how things get done, in how people operate.
This section gives Claude a sense of their character, not just their workflow.]

## Current Focus

[What they're working toward right now — goals, projects, priorities. The context Claude needs
to understand what matters most to them in this season of their work.]
```

## BUSINESS.md Template

```markdown
# BUSINESS.md — [Business Name]

> Last updated: [today's date]

## What We Do

[2–3 paragraph description. What the business is, what it does, who it serves, what makes
it different. Positioning-aware. Should sound like the brand, not a Wikipedia entry.]

## Mission and Core Beliefs

[The driving belief or mission. What they stand for. What they're pushing against.
Written with conviction — this should be the brand's north star.]

## Products and Services

[For each product/service: name, description, who it's for, price point, customer outcome,
what makes it different. Full paragraphs — not just a name and price.]

## Brand Voice

### Personality
[The three words expanded into actual guidance. How the brand sounds, feels, comes across.
Specific enough to guide writing decisions.]

### Key Messages
[The 3–5 core messages or beliefs that run through all content. These are the ideas
Claude always comes back to regardless of content type.]

### What We Avoid
[Topics, tones, comparisons, and associations the brand steers clear of. Specific enough
to be actionable.]

## Content Strategy

### Channels and Frequency
[All active publishing platforms with notes on content type, frequency, and role.]

### Content's Role in the Business
[How content fits — top of funnel, community building, direct sales, thought leadership, etc.]

### What Works
[Patterns from content that has performed well. Topics, formats, angles that resonate.]

## Competitive Position

[How they're positioned relative to others. What they're different from.
What they don't want to be associated with.]
```

## ICP.md Template

```markdown
# ICP.md — Ideal Customer Profile

> Last updated: [today's date]

## Who They Are

[A vivid, specific description of the ideal customer as a real person. Professional situation,
daily life, life stage. Not a demographic — a human being. 1–2 paragraphs.]

## Their Situation

[Where they are right now. The problem they're dealing with, how long they've had it,
what they've already tried, why it hasn't worked. Written with empathy — this should make
the ideal customer feel deeply understood.]

## What They Want

### The Surface Outcome
[What they say they want — the practical result.]

### The Real Desire
[What they actually want underneath — the life change, the feeling, the deeper transformation.]

### What Success Looks Like
[Specific picture of their life or business after the problem is solved.]

## Their Fears and Objections

[What holds them back. The fears they have, the objections they raise, the stories they tell
themselves. Specific and honest — not softened or sanitized.]

## Their Language

### How They Describe the Problem
[Exact phrases and words they use — their language, not marketing language.
This section gets quoted directly in content.]

### What They Call Themselves
[How they refer to their role, situation, or identity.]

### Language That Resonates
[Words, phrases, and framing that makes them feel understood and spoken to directly.]

### Language That Turns Them Off
[Words and framing that feel like it's not for them or feels inauthentic to their world.]

## What Makes Them Buy

[What finally tips them from interested to committed. The combination of trust signals,
proof, and messaging that converts them. Where they spend time online.
What they need to see before they pull out a credit card.]
```
